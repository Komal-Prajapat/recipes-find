LADOO

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup of Besan (gram flour)
  2. 1/4 cup of Ghee (clarified butter)
  3. 1/2 cup of Powdered Sugar
  4. 1/4 teaspoon of Cardamom Powder
  5. 2 tablespoons of Chopped Nuts (e.g., almonds, cashews)
  6. A pinch of Saffron strands (optional)
  7. A pinch of Salt

Instructions:

  1. Roasting Besan :
    • Heat a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai on low flame and add the besan.
    • Dry roast the besan while continuously stirring to avoid any lumps and to ensure even roasting.
    • Roast for about 10-15 minutes or until it turns fragrant and slightly changes color to a golden brown. Be cautious not to over-roast it, as it can give a bitter taste.
    • Turn off the heat and allow the roasted besan to cool.
  2. Prepare the Ghee:
    • In another pan, melt the ghee over low heat. You can also add a pinch of saffron strands to the ghee for flavor and color. Allow the ghee to cool slightly.
  3. Combine Besan and Ghee:
    • Once the besan has cooled down and the ghee is warm (not hot), slowly pour the ghee over the roasted besan while continuously stirring to mix them thoroughly. Ensure there are no lumps.
  4. Add Sugar and Nuts:
    • Let the mixture cool for a few minutes, and then add powdered sugar, cardamom powder, chopped nuts, and raisins (if using). Mix everything well.
  5. Shape into Ladoos:
    • While the mixture is still warm, take small portions of it and shape them into round balls or ladoos with your hands. You can make them as big or small as you prefer. If the mixture cools down and becomes difficult to shape, you can reheat it slightly.
    • Let the ladoos cool completely, and they will firm up as they cool.

DAL BATTI

Ingredients for Baati:

For the Baati (makes approximately 10 baatis):
2 cups Whole wheat flour (atta)
1/2 cup Semolina (sooji)
1/2 cup Ghee (clarified butter)
1/2 cup Milk
1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (jeera)
Salt to taste
Water (for kneading)

Ingredients for Dal:

For the Dal: 1 cup Toor dal (split pigeon pea lentils)
1/2 cup Chana dal (split Bengal gram lentils)
1 Onion, finely chopped
2 Tomatoes, chopped
2-3 Green chilies, chopped
1 teaspoon Ginger-garlic paste
1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
1 teaspoon Red chili powder
1 teaspoon Cumin seeds (jeera)
1/2 teaspoon Asafoetida (hing)
2-3 tablespoons Ghee (clarified butter)
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:
For Baati:
  • Prepare the Baati Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, semolina, cumin seeds, and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
    • Add ghee and milk to the dry ingredients. Begin to knead the dough.
    • Add water as needed to form a stiff dough. Knead it for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • Shape and Bake Baatis:
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C).
    • Divide the dough into equal portions and shape them into round balls (baatis).
    • Place the baatis on a baking tray and bake for about 30-40 minutes or until they turn golden brown. You can also deep-fry the baatis if you prefer.
  • Prepare the Tadka (Tempering):
    • In a separate pan, heat ghee. Add cumin seeds and asafoetida.
    • Add chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  • Add Tomatoes and Spices:
    • Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil begins to separate.
  • Combine with Cooked Lentils:
    • Add the cooked lentils to the tempering and bring the dal to a boil. Adjust the consistency by adding water as needed.
  • Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish the dal with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with the baatis.

    • MAKKI ROTI

    • Ingredients:
      • 1 cup Makki ka Atta (cornmeal)
      • Warm water, as needed
      • Salt to taste
      • Ghee or butter (for serving, optional)
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Dough:**
        • In a mixing bowl, take the makki ka atta (cornmeal) and add a pinch of salt.
        • Slowly add warm water while mixing until the dough comes together. The dough should be soft and pliable, but not too sticky. Knead it for a few minutes until it's smooth.
      2. **Divide and Shape the Rotis:**
        • Divide the dough into small, golf ball-sized portions.
        • Take one portion and flatten it between your palms to create a small disk. Place it on a clean, dry surface.
        • Use your fingers to gently pat and shape the dough into a round roti. Makki rotis are typically thicker than wheat rotis.
      3. **Cook the Rotis:**
        • Heat a griddle or tava on medium-high heat. It's essential to use a hot griddle for makki roti.
        • Carefully lift the shaped makki roti and place it on the hot griddle. Cook it for a few minutes on one side until you see small bubbles forming on the surface.
        • Flip the roti and cook the other side until it's golden brown and slightly crisp. You can add a little ghee or butter on top while cooking, if desired.
      4. **Serve Hot:**
        • Remove the makki roti from the griddle and place it on a plate. Serve it hot with a dollop of ghee or butter. It's traditionally served with sarson da saag or any curry of your choice.

    DAL MAKHANU

    • Ingredients:
      • 1 cup whole urad dal (black gram)
      • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
      • 4 cups water (for soaking)
      • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
      • 1 large onion, finely chopped
      • 2 tomatoes, pureed
      • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
      • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
      • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
      • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
      • 1 teaspoon garam masala
      • Salt to taste
      • 1/4 cup fresh cream
      • 2 tablespoons butter
      • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
    • Instructions:
      1. **Soak the Lentils:**
        • Rinse the urad dal and kidney beans under running water. Soak them in water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
        • Drain and rinse the soaked lentils before using them.
      2. **Cook the Lentils:**
        • In a pressure cooker, add the soaked and drained urad dal and kidney beans along with 4 cups of water. Pressure cook for about 6-7 whistles or until they are soft and fully cooked.
        • Mash the cooked lentils slightly with the back of a spoon and set aside.
      3. **Prepare the Gravy:**
        • In a large pan, heat ghee. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
        • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
        • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
        • Stir in the tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the ghee separates from the tomato mixture.
      4. **Combine Dal and Gravy:**
        • Add the cooked dal to the tomato-onion gravy. Mix well and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together.
        • Stir in fresh cream and simmer for an additional 5 minutes. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
      5. **Garnish and Serve:**
        • Before serving, garnish the Dal Makhani with a drizzle of butter and fresh coriander leaves.
        • Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.

    CHOLE BHATURE

    • Ingredients:
      • For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
        • 1 cup dried chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight
        • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
        • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
        • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
        • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
        • 1/2 cup cooking oil
        • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
        • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
        • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
        • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
        • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
        • 1 teaspoon garam masala
        • Salt to taste
        • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
      • For Bhature (Fried Bread):
        • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
        • 1/2 cup yogurt
        • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
        • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
        • 1/4 teaspoon salt
        • Oil for deep frying
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Chole:**
        • Drain the soaked chickpeas and cook them in a pressure cooker with enough water until they are soft. It usually takes about 4-5 whistles.
        • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
        • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
        • Stir in chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates from the mixture.
        • Add the cooked chickpeas, along with some water, and let the curry simmer for 10-15 minutes. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
      2. **Prepare the Bhature Dough:**
        • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, yogurt, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Knead the mixture into a smooth dough. Add a little water if necessary.
        • Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 2 hours.
      3. **Fry the Bhature:**
        • Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into round discs using a rolling pin.
        • Heat oil in a deep frying pan. When the oil is hot, carefully slide in the rolled bhature and fry until they puff up and turn golden brown on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.
      4. **Serve:**
        • Serve hot Chole with Bhature along with some chopped onions, lemon wedges, and your favorite pickles.

    SAMOSA

    • Ingredients:
      • For the Samosa Dough:
        • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
        • 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
        • 1/2 teaspoon salt
        • Water, as needed
      • For the Samosa Filling:
        • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
        • 1/2 cup boiled and chopped green peas
        • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
        • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
        • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
        • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
        • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
        • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
        • 1 teaspoon garam masala
        • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
        • Salt to taste
        • Oil for deep frying
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Samosa Dough:**
        • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, ghee (or oil), and salt. Mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
        • Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a firm, smooth dough. Cover it and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
      2. **Prepare the Samosa Filling:**
        • In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
        • Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
        • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
        • Stir in boiled and mashed potatoes, boiled green peas, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the filling is well combined and fragrant.
        • Add chopped coriander leaves and let the filling cool.
      3. **Shape and Fill the Samosas:**
        • Divide the rested dough into equal portions and roll them into balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval shape and cut it into two halves to make two semi-circles.
        • Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge using a little water to form a triangular pocket.
        • Fill the pocket with the prepared filling and seal the open edge to form a triangular samosa. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
      4. **Fry the Samosas:**
        • Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Carefully slide in the prepared samosas and deep fry them until they turn golden brown and crisp. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
      5. **Serve:**
        • Serve hot samosas with tamarind chutney or mint chutney.

    Risotto

    • Ingredients:
      • 1 cup Arborio rice
      • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
      • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
      • 1 small onion, finely chopped
      • 2 cloves garlic, minced
      • 2 tablespoons olive oil
      • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
      • 2 tablespoons butter
      • Salt and pepper to taste
      • Fresh herbs (such as parsley or basil) for garnish
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Broth:**
        • In a separate saucepan, heat the chicken or vegetable broth over low heat. Keep it warm but not boiling.
      2. **Sauté the Aromatics:**
        • In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
        • Add the minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
      3. **Toast the Rice:**
        • Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir to coat it with the oil. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the rice turns translucent at the edges.
        • If desired, pour in the white wine and stir until it's mostly absorbed by the rice.
      4. **Cook the Risotto:**
        • Begin adding the warm broth one ladle at a time to the rice. Stir constantly and allow the liquid to be absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and cooked to your preferred level of doneness (usually about 18-20 minutes).
        • Season with salt and pepper to taste. The risotto should be creamy, not dry or too soupy.
      5. **Finish the Risotto:**
        • Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and butter until well combined.
        • Cover the pan and let the risotto rest for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
      6. **Serve:**
        • Garnish the risotto with fresh herbs and additional Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

    Lasagna

    • Ingredients:
      • 9 lasagna noodles
      • 1 pound (450g) ground beef or ground Italian sausage
      • 1 small onion, chopped
      • 2 cloves garlic, minced
      • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
      • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
      • 2 teaspoons dried basil
      • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
      • Salt and pepper to taste
      • 3 cups ricotta cheese
      • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
      • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
      • 1 egg, beaten
      • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)
    • Instructions:
      1. **Cook the Noodles:**
        • Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and set aside.
      2. **Prepare the Meat Sauce:**
        • In a large skillet, cook the ground beef or Italian sausage over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
        • Add chopped onions and minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for a few minutes until the onions are softened.
        • Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
      3. **Prepare the Cheese Filling:**
        • In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, 1 1/2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, and the beaten egg. Mix well.
      4. **Assemble the Lasagna:**
        • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
        • In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce to prevent sticking.
        • Place three lasagna noodles on the bottom of the dish.
        • Spread a layer of the cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of meat sauce. Repeat these layers until all ingredients are used up, finishing with a layer of meat sauce on top.
        • Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
      5. **Bake and Serve:**
        • Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes or until the lasagna is hot and bubbling, and the top is golden brown.
        • Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired.

    Tiramisu

    • Ingredients:
      • 6 egg yolks
      • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
      • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
      • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
      • 1 cup brewed espresso or strong coffee, cooled
      • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (e.g., Kahlúa) (optional)
      • 1 package (7 ounces) ladyfingers (savoiardi biscuits)
      • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
      • Chocolate shavings or cocoa powder (for garnish, optional)
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Mascarpone Mixture:**
        • In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and granulated sugar. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and continue to whisk until the mixture is pale, thick, and slightly foamy. This should take about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
        • Once the egg mixture has cooled, gently fold in the mascarpone cheese until well combined.
      2. **Whip the Heavy Cream:**
        • In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to over-whip the cream.
        • Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until smooth and well incorporated. Set aside.
      3. **Assemble the Tiramisu:**
        • In a shallow dish, combine the brewed espresso and coffee liqueur (if using).
        • Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, making sure not to soak them too long to avoid sogginess.
        • Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers at the bottom of your serving dish. You can use a trifle dish, a square or rectangular dish, or individual serving glasses.
        • Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.
      4. **Chill and Garnish:**
        • Cover the dish and refrigerate the tiramisu for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld and the dessert to set.
        • Before serving, dust the top with unsweetened cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings or additional cocoa powder if desired.

    Gelato

    • Ingredients:
      • 2 cups whole milk
      • 1 cup heavy cream
      • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
      • 4 large egg yolks
      • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
      • Additional flavorings (e.g., chocolate chips, fruit puree, nuts) as desired
    • Instructions:
      1. **Prepare the Custard:**
        • In a saucepan, combine the whole milk and heavy cream. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a temperature just below boiling (about 170°F or 77°C). Remove it from the heat and set it aside.
        • In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and granulated sugar until the mixture becomes pale and slightly thick. This creates the custard base.
        • Slowly pour a small amount of the hot milk mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to temper the eggs. Gradually add the rest of the milk mixture, continuing to whisk until well combined.
        • Return the custard mixture to the saucepan and heat it over low heat, stirring continuously. Cook until the mixture thickens slightly, and it can coat the back of a spoon. Do not allow it to boil. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
        • Remove the custard from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let it cool to room temperature. You can also add any additional flavorings or mix-ins at this stage, such as chocolate chips or fruit puree.
      2. **Chill the Custard:**
        • Cover the custard and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours or overnight to chill thoroughly. This step is essential for the gelato to have the right texture.
      3. **Churn the Gelato:**
        • Pour the chilled custard into an ice cream maker and churn it according to the manufacturer's instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes, and the gelato should have a soft, creamy texture.
        • If you want to add any additional mix-ins, like nuts or more chocolate chips, add them during the last few minutes of churning.
      4. **Freeze the Gelato:**
        • Transfer the churned gelato to an airtight container and freeze it for an additional 2-3 hours or until it firms up to your desired consistency.
      5. **Serve:**
        • Scoop the gelato into serving dishes, and enjoy your homemade gelato! You can garnish it with fresh fruits, nuts, or chocolate shavings if you like.

    SAMOSA

    Ingredients:

    1. 1 liter of Cow's Milk (full-fat)
    2. 1-2 tablespoons of Lemon Juice or White Vinegar
    3. 1 cup of Sugar
    4. 4-5 cups of Water
    5. A few strands of Saffron (optional)
    6. 1/2 teaspoon of Cardamom Powder

    Instructions:

    1. Prepare Chenna (Curdled Milk):
      • Boil the milk and add lemon juice or white vinegar to curdle it.
      • Strain the curdled milk to separate chenna (milk solids) from whey.
    2. Knead the Chenna:
      • Knead the chenna until it becomes smooth and lump-free.
    3. Shape Rasgulla Balls:
      • Divide the chenna into small, equal-sized portions.
      • Roll them into smooth, crack-free balls.
    4. Prepare Sugar Syrup:
      • In a pan, combine sugar and water to make the sugar syrup.
      • Add saffron strands and cardamom powder to enhance the flavor.
    5. Cook the Rasgullas:
      • Gently place the rasgulla balls into the simmering sugar syrup.
      • Cover and cook for about 15-20 minutes until they puff up and float.
    6. Cool and Serve:
      • In a pan, combine sugar and water to make the sugar syrup.
      • They will absorb the syrup and become spongy and sweet.
      • Serve at room temperature or chilled.
